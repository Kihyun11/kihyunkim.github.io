About Me

I'm a Machine Learning and Computer Vision researcher with a MEng in Electrical and Information Engineering from Imperial College London. My research focuses on tiny object detection in aerial images, where I've designed novel attention mechanisms to push the boundaries of what modern CNN-based detectors can achieve.

Currently serving as a Research Scientist at the ROK Army Training & Doctrine Command, I developed the Zoom-Attention and TODAM modules — integrated into a CNN architecture that surpassed YOLOv8 and CBAM-based baselines. This work is under review for ICASSP 2026.

I'm passionate about applying AI to real-world problems, from drone-based surveillance to autonomous robotics and AI companions.
